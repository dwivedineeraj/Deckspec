# Composite and PVC Decking Maintenance Guide: Keep Your Deck Looking New

The biggest selling point of composite and PVC decking is low maintenance. But "low maintenance" doesn't mean "no maintenance." With the right care, your deck will stay beautiful for decades. Neglect it, and even premium boards can develop stains, mold, and premature fading.

This guide covers how to maintain composite and PVC decking from [Trex](/brands/trex), [TimberTech](/brands/timbertech), [Fiberon](/brands/fiberon), [Deckorators](/brands/deckorators), and [MoistureShield](/brands/moistureshield).

## Routine Cleaning: Every 2–4 Months

For most decks, a simple cleaning every few months is enough:

1. **Sweep away debris** — Leaves, dirt, and pollen trap moisture against the boards. Use a broom or leaf blower.
2. **Rinse with a garden hose** — Remove surface dust and pollen. Use a standard spray nozzle, not a pressure washer.
3. **Mild soap solution** — Mix warm water with a few drops of dish soap or a brand-specific deck cleaner. Scrub with a soft-bristle brush.
4. **Rinse thoroughly** — Soap residue can attract dirt. Hose off completely.
5. **Let dry** — Air dry is best. Avoid walking on wet decking with shoes that may track dirt.

## Deep Cleaning: Twice a Year (Spring and Fall)

In spring, clean off winter's accumulation of salt, grime, and mildew. In fall, remove leaves and pollen before wet season sets in.

**For composite decking** (Trex, TimberTech composite lines, Fiberon composite, Deckorators, MoistureShield):
- Use a manufacturer-recommended cleaner (Trex Cleaner, TimberTech Deck Cleaner, or oxygen bleach like OxiClean)
- Apply with a pump sprayer, let sit for 5–10 minutes (don't let it dry)
- Scrub with a soft-bristle brush or deck brush on a pole
- Rinse thoroughly with a hose

**For PVC decking** (Azek Vintage, Azek Landmark, Fiberon Paramount):
- PVC is more chemically resistant than composite
- Use the same mild cleaner or a dedicated PVC cleaner
- Avoid abrasive scrubbers — they can dull the surface finish

### Can You Use a Pressure Washer?

Use extreme caution. A pressure washer can damage the cap layer, leaving permanent marks.

- If you must use one: Stay at least 12" from the board surface
- Use a 40° or 60° nozzle (fan spray, not zero-degree)
- Keep pressure under 1,500 PSI
- Never use a turbo nozzle or surface cleaner attachment

Better to skip the pressure washer and use a garden hose with a spray nozzle. It's slower but won't void your warranty.

## Stain Removal

Even with capped decking, stains can happen. Act quickly for best results.

| Stain Type | Treatment | For Composite | For PVC |
|------------|-----------|---------------|---------|
| Food/drink | Warm water + mild soap | ✓ | ✓ |
| Grease/oil | Degreaser or dish soap | ✓ | ✓ |
| Wine/coffee | Oxygen bleach solution | ✓ | ✓ |
| Rust (from furniture) | Oxalic acid cleaner | ✓ | ⚠️ Test first |
| Leaf tannins | Composite cleaner | ✓ | ✓ |
| Paint/marker | Mineral spirits or rubbing alcohol | ⚠️ Test first | ✓ |
| Mold/mildew | 50/50 vinegar-water or bleach solution | ✓ | ✓ |

Never use acetone, MEK, or paint thinners on composite decking. These can dissolve or damage the cap layer.

## Mold and Mildew Prevention

Modern capped composites are resistant to mold, but in shaded, humid environments, organic matter (pollen, leaf debris) can feed mold growth on the board surface.

**Prevention:**
- Keep the deck swept — especially in shady areas
- Trim nearby bushes to improve airflow
- Ensure proper drainage — standing water encourages growth
- Consider lighter colors for shaded decks (they show dirt less and stay cooler)

**Removal:**
- Mix 1 cup of bleach per gallon of water (or use a brand-approved mold cleaner)
- Apply with a sprayer, let sit for 10 minutes
- Scrub gently with a soft brush
- Rinse thoroughly

## Preventing Scratches

Capped composite decking is durable but not scratch-proof. While [Trex Signature](/brands/trex/signature) and [TimberTech Vintage](/brands/timbertech/vintage) have excellent scratch resistance, all decks benefit from care:

- Use felt pads under furniture legs
- Lift chairs and grills — don't drag them across the deck
- Keep pet nails trimmed
- Avoid dragging heavy pots, toolboxes, or sharp objects

If scratches do occur, they often fade over time as the UV-resistant cap layer self-blends. Deep scratches through the cap layer may require board replacement.

## Seasonal Care Tips

**Winter:**
- Keep the deck clear of snow. Use a plastic shovel (never metal)
- Don't use ice melt containing calcium chloride — it can damage the cap. Potassium chloride or urea-based products are safer
- Salt tracked from sidewalks can leave white residue. Rinse the deck after thaw

**Summer:**
- Dark colors absorb heat. Walking barefoot on a dark composite deck in full sun can be uncomfortable
- Consider heat-mitigating products like [Trex Transcend Lineage](/brands/trex/transcend-lineage) (SunComfortable) or [TimberTech](/brands/timbertech) Cool Touch Technology PVC
- Light-colored boards (grays, tans, beiges) stay significantly cooler than dark browns and charcoal

## Maintaining Railing and Fascia

Don't forget the rest of the deck:

- **Railing**: Wipe down composite or aluminum railing with soap and water. Check for loose balusters seasonally.
- **Fascia boards**: Same cleaning as deck boards. Check for gaps opened by expansion/contraction.
- **Underneath**: Check for pest activity, moisture buildup, or settling posts annually.

## When to Call for Help

Most deck maintenance is DIY-friendly, but call a professional if you see:
- Lifting or buckling boards (sign of improper installation or inadequate expansion gaps)
- Peeling or delaminating cap layer (manufacturing defect — file a warranty claim)
- Soft spots or rot in supporting structure
- Large areas of mold that don't respond to cleaning

## The Bottom Line

Composite and PVC decking maintenance boils down to three things: sweep regularly, clean with soap and water twice a year, and treat stains immediately. That's it. Compared to the annual staining, sealing, and sanding that pressure-treated wood requires, the time savings are enormous.
