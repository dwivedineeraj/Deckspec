# Trex vs TimberTech vs Fiberon: Which Decking Brand Is Best?

If you're shopping for composite decking in 2026, you'll quickly narrow your search to the three biggest names: **Trex**, **TimberTech**, and **Fiberon**. Together they account for the majority of North American composite and PVC decking sales. But picking between them isn't straightforward — all three overlap on price, warranties, and features.

Here's how they actually stack up.

## Trex: The Market Leader

Trex is the largest wood-alternative decking manufacturer in the world, founded in 1996 and headquartered in Winchester, Virginia. Their products use 95% recycled materials — reclaimed wood and recycled plastic film — making them the most eco-friendly choice at every price tier.

**Product line breakdown:**
- **[Trex Enhance](/brands/trex/enhance)** ($4–$7/sq ft): Entry-level capped composite with a scalloped profile for lighter weight. 12 colors across Basics and Naturals sub-lines. 25-year warranty.
- **[Trex Select](/brands/trex/select)** ($6–$8/sq ft): Mid-range solid-core composite with a curated 5-color palette. 25-year warranty.
- **[Trex Transcend](/brands/trex/transcend)** ($8–$11/sq ft): Premium deep wood-grain composite with excellent scratch and stain resistance. 12 colors. 25-year warranty.
- **[Trex Transcend Lineage](/brands/trex/transcend-lineage)** ($10–$13/sq ft): Ultra-premium with heat-mitigating SunComfortable technology and linear grain. 50-year warranty.
- **[Trex Signature](/brands/trex/signature)** ($13–$17/sq ft): Top-tier luxury composite with photorealistic woodgrain and the highest scratch resistance. 50-year warranty.

**Best for:** Eco-conscious buyers, homeowners wanting the widest color selection, and anyone who values brand recognition for resale.

## TimberTech: Premium Aesthetics

TimberTech (owned by Azek Building Products) offers both capped composite (TimberTech PRO) and premium PVC (Azek) decking. Their standout feature is unmatched wood-grain realism — particularly in the Azek Vintage line.

**Product line breakdown:**
- **[TimberTech Terrain](/brands/timbertech/terrain)** ($5–$7/sq ft): Entry capped composite, scalloped profile. 25-year warranty.
- **[TimberTech Terrain+](/brands/timbertech/terrain-plus)** ($6–$8/sq ft): Improved colors and subtle grain. 30-year warranty.
- **[TimberTech Legacy](/brands/timbertech/legacy)** ($8–$10/sq ft): Premium composite with hand-scraped, artisanal finish. 40-year warranty.
- **[TimberTech Reserve](/brands/timbertech/reserve)** ($8–$10/sq ft): Reclaimed-wood look composite. 40-year warranty.
- **[Azek Harvest](/brands/timbertech/harvest)** ($7–$9/sq ft): Entry-PVC with cathedral grain. 50-year warranty.
- **[Azek Landmark](/brands/timbertech/landmark)** ($10–$13/sq ft): Premium PVC crosscut hardwood look. 50-year warranty.
- **[Azek Vintage](/brands/timbertech/vintage)** ($12–$16/sq ft): Flagship PVC with wire-brushed exotic hardwood finish, Cool Touch Technology, and Class A fire rating. 50-year warranty.

**Best for:** Homeowners who want the most realistic wood look, need fire-rated materials for WUI zones, or have moisture-prone sites where PVC excels.

## Fiberon: Best Bang for the Buck

Fiberon, based in New London, North Carolina, manufactures both composite and PVC decking with the highest recycled content in the industry (94% across most lines). They consistently undercut Trex and TimberTech by $1–$2/sq ft at comparable tiers.

**Product line breakdown:**
- **[Fiberon Concordia](/brands/fiberon/concordia)** ($2.80–$3.50/sq ft): Entry-level capped composite. 25-year warranty.
- **[Fiberon Good Life](/brands/fiberon/good-life)** ($3.20–$4/sq ft): Value composite with three-sided capping. 30-year warranty. Available at Lowe's.
- **[Fiberon Promenade](/brands/fiberon/promenade)** ($4.50–$5.50/sq ft): Mid-range solid-core composite. 35-year warranty.
- **[Fiberon ArmorGuard](/brands/fiberon/armorguard)** ($5–$6/sq ft): Premium composite with enhanced scratch resistance. 40-year warranty.
- **[Fiberon Sanctuary](/brands/fiberon/sanctuary)** ($5.50–$6.50/sq ft): Premium multi-tonal composite. 40-year warranty.
- **[Fiberon Paramount](/brands/fiberon/paramount)** ($7.50–$9/sq ft): Premium capped PVC with Lifetime structural warranty and Class A fire rating.

**Best for:** Budget-conscious buyers who still want capped composite, value-seekers who want the most recycled content, and projects where every dollar counts.

## Which Brand Should You Choose?

**Choose Trex if** you value the largest color selection, prefer the most eco-friendly manufacturing, or want the widest availability through Home Depot. Trex Enhance at $4–$7/sq ft is the best-selling entry-level composite for good reason.

**Choose TimberTech/Azek if** you want the most realistic wood grain on the market, need PVC's superior moisture resistance, or require fire-rated decking for wildfire-prone areas. The Azek Vintage line's wire-brushed finish is unmatched.

**Choose Fiberon if** budget is your primary concern but you won't compromise on capping. Fiberon Concordia at $2.80/sq ft is the cheapest capped composite available, and the Good Life line at $3.20/sq ft outperforms comparably-priced competitors on recycled content.

All three brands are excellent. Your decision should come down to which specific product line fits your budget, aesthetic preference, and performance requirements — not the brand name on the box.
